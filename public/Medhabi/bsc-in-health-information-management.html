About B.Sc in Health Information Management

This programme teaches about the management of health information systems consistent with medical, administrative, ethical and legal requirements of the health care delivery system. The profession combines knowledge of health care systems and business administration. The health information administrator is responsible for the management of health information systems consistent with the medical, administrative, ethical and legal requirements of the health care delivery system.
B.Sc in Health Information Management is designed to equip students to take up independent middle level managerial positions, interacting with all levels of healthcare organizations and use patient data in decision making and everyday operations.Our graduates work in a broad range of settings that span the continuum of healthcare including hospitals, physician practices, nursing homes, home health agencies, long term care facilities and public health agencies.

Job Opportunities

A patient care coordinator, also known as a patient information coordinator, explains a patient's health care treatments to them and serves as a liaison between patients and providers.

This is an intriguing option for those who have an interest in research. Their job role includes identifying patients, tracking inventory, collecting patient data, overseeing protocols etc.

A health information technician keeps patient records confidential by complying with privacy regulations. They maintain efficient records systems and work with computers to process information.
